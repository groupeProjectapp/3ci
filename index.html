ABOUT US
       Welcome to the CyberSecurity and Cyber Intelligence Club at ENSA Tangier! Whether you're new to cybersecurity or an experienced pro, our club offers the perfect environment to learn, share knowledge, and collaborate. Engage in hands-on workshops, seminars, CTF competitions, and hackathons that keep you at the cutting edge of digital security. We also explore AI, machine learning, and deep learning, which are revolutionizing cybersecurity. From AI-driven security solutions to machine learning threat detection, our projects and competitions will ignite your curiosity and enhance your skills. Join us and shape the future of cybersecurity and AI while building a safer digital world!

       Ethical Hacking Workshops: Detailed Overview

Web Attacks Simulation

Join our hands-on workshop focused on simulating a range of web-based attacks using the Kali Linux distribution. Participants will gain practical experience in executing and understanding common web vulnerabilities such as:

  • SQL Injection
  • XSS (Cross-Site Scripting) Attacks
  • Dictionary Attacks
  • CSRF (Cross-Site Request Forgery) Attacks
  • Phishing Techniques
  • Local File Inclusion (LFI)

Penetration Testing Simulation

This workshop is designed to simulate intrusions and penetration testing on vulnerable machines using Kali Linux. It offers participants the opportunity to explore real-world attack scenarios and practice the skills needed to identify and exploit vulnerabilities.

Artificial Intelligence in Cyber Attack Modeling

Discover how AI techniques can be leveraged to model and simulate cyber-attacks. This workshop targets the cutting-edge intersection of artificial intelligence and cybersecurity, providing insights into AI-driven attack strategies and defense mechanisms.

Theme

  • AI (Artificial Intelligence)
  • Web Pentesting
  • Social Engineering

       Ethical Hacking Days: Detailed Overview

Event Schedule

Ethical Hacking Days, Second Edition, is a 3-day event running from November 29, 2024, to December 1, 2024. This event features conferences and a Capture the Flag (CTF) competition on the second day. Participants will engage in real-world challenges including:

  • Cryptography
  • Exploitation (Pwn)
  • Web Pentesting
  • Reverse Engineering

CTF Competition

The CTF competition will provide hands-on experience with various security challenges and practical scenarios, allowing participants to test their skills and knowledge in a competitive environment.

Networking and Experience Sharing

Participants will have the opportunity to connect with top professionals in the cybersecurity market, share experiences, and gain insights from industry experts.

Theme

  • Cyber Security
  • Web Pentesting
  • Post Exploitation
